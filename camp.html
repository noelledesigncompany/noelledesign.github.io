Why Attend a Public Speaking Camp?

  1. Public speaking is an indispensable life skill that has also been named as one of the greatest phobias in the United States. Conquering this anxiety at a young age can help students for the rest of their lives.
  2. Public speaking teaches skills that are vital to success in both high school and college. These include researching, organizing, analyzing, synthesizing as well as listening and critical thinking skills.
  3. The National Association of College and Employers found that the ability to communicate effectively is the quality most employers want to see in new recruits.
  4. Becoming comfortable with public speaking can help improve self-confidence, social skills as well as interpersonal communication skills.


Schedule

Day 1: Public Speaking 101
Students will learn basic public speaking and communication skills and will get hands on experience with different styles of public speaking.

Day 2: Research and Argumentation
Students will be introduced to the communication theories associated with effective persuasive speaking as well as methods of gathering and presenting research to support claims.

Day 3: Interpretation an Improvisation
Students will explore different areas of acting, including interpretation of literature and improvisation.

Day 4: Presentation and Showcase
Students will work on polishing presentation skills in the speaking style of their choice. Students will end the camp with a mini-showcase where they will have the opportunity to present in front of an audience of parents and peers.


FAQs

Will there be a break for lunch for students in the full day session?
Campers who are attending the full day are encouraged to bring their own lunch. Pizza, candy and refreshments will be available for sale during lunch.

What do students who attend the full day session do for the afternoon?
Students who attend the full day session will follow the morning session for the first half of the day and perform extension activities for the second half of the day.
